GROVER

Version 1

The all-terrain six-wheeled ground rover is a remarkable feat of engineering, meticulously crafted to navigate a multitude of landscapes and challenges with ease. Each division within our dedicated team plays an integral role in bringing this visionary rover to life, pooling their expertise in mechanical design, software development, and rigorous testing. This collaborative synergy is at the heart of our mission, propelling us towards the realization of this groundbreaking project. As we continue to work in unison, we're not just building a rover; we're forging the future of all-terrain mobility and redefining the possibilities of exploration and innovation in the field of robotics.

Mechanical

  • Our Mechanical subdivision is focused on optimizing the suspension parameters and improving the joints to reduce play, ensuring the rover's stability and maneuverability across diverse terrains.

  • To achieve this, we are utilizing aluminum for the suspension components, known for its lightweight and durability. The chassis framework incorporates aluminum extrusion rails, providing a sturdy and adaptable base for the rover's structure.

  • Our team is diligently using CAD modeling techniques to fine-tune the suspension system. This involves precise adjustments to parameters such as spring rates, damping coefficients, and shock absorber angles. By optimizing these aspects, we aim to enhance the rover's ability to absorb shocks and maintain traction, ultimately improving its off-road capabilities.

  • To eliminate any undesirable play in the suspension and joints, our mechanical experts are employing advanced CAD modeling techniques. By meticulously refining the design and tolerances of critical components, we ensure that the rover's movements are precise and responsive, even in rugged terrains.

Electrical

  • The electrical division is responsible for ensuring the rover's sensors and encoders are seamlessly integrated into the system. This involves microcontroller programming, wiring, and electronics design to facilitate accurate data acquisition and control.

  • Our team is actively interfacing various sensors and encoders with the rover's microcontroller. This integration allows us to gather real-time data on factors such as terrain conditions, speed, and position, enabling precise control and navigation.

  • We are developing intricate software algorithms to enable the microcontroller to process sensor data efficiently. This programming is essential for making informed decisions regarding the rover's movement and behavior.

Software

  • The software subdivision is dedicated to making the rover autonomous using a Pixhawk flight controller and implementing high-level control, including obstacle avoidance.

  • Our software engineers are working tirelessly to program the rover to operate autonomously. The Pixhawk flight controller, renowned for its reliability, is central to this endeavor. It will enable the rover to follow predefined paths and make real-time adjustments based on sensor data.

  • Ensuring the rover's safety and efficiency, our software team is developing obstacle avoidance algorithms. These algorithms will allow the rover to detect and navigate around obstacles, enhancing its adaptability in unpredictable environments.

  • Team Gensus is making remarkable progress in the development of our all-terrain six-wheeled ground rover. With our mechanical division fine-tuning the suspension and chassis, our electrical division seamlessly integrating sensors and encoders, and our software division working on autonomy and obstacle avoidance, we are well on our way to creating a versatile and capable rover for various applications.
